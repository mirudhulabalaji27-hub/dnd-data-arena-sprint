## ❓ Problem Statement

Most music streaming platforms struggle to understand **why free users don't convert to paid plans** and **at what point loyal users form behavioral habits**. This project analyzes real Spotify user data to:

- Identify distinct behavioral segments among users
- Discover peak engagement windows
- Uncover untapped Premium conversion opportunities
- Determine the critical retention timeline

> **Core Question:** What behavioral patterns separate loyal Spotify users from drop-off risks, and where are the biggest revenue opportunities hiding?

## 💡 Key Insights

### 1. 🌙 60% of Users Listen at Night
Night is Spotify's single most powerful engagement window. Playlist targeting, push notifications, and curated drops should be concentrated between **9 PM – 1 AM** to maximize reach.

### 2. 💸 35.8% Are Willing to Pay — But Haven't
**186 users** have expressed intent to go Premium but remain on the free tier. This is the highest-ROI conversion segment — price friction, not desire, is the barrier. A targeted 3-month discounted trial could unlock this group immediately.

### 3. 📅 Loyalty Snowballs After 6 Months
User retention grows steadily with time — **169 users have stayed 2+ years**. However, the first 6 months are the highest drop-off window (91 at-risk users). Early onboarding investment is the single most impactful retention lever.

### 4. 🎵 Music Dominates, But Teens Split Evenly
78.8% of all users prefer music — but the **12–20 age group** splits almost evenly between music and podcasts, signaling a growing podcast audience that is currently underserved in targeting strategies.

### 5. 😌 Relaxation Is the #1 Mood Across All Genders
The dominant listening mood across every gender segment is **Relaxation**, with female users leading every mood category. Mood-based playlist personalization (especially relaxation/stress-relief) has the highest engagement potential.
